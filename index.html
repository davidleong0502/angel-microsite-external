3 Key Habits

Just 3 Habits to Guide You On Your Journey

Getting started with A&GEL is about more than just typing a question and hitting enter in Chat. To truly harness the power of AI tools like A&GEL, you need to shift how you think about interacting with AI.

Habit 01

Give A&GEL Something to Work With

The most common mistake new users make is underestimating the importance of giving AI tools sufficient information to work with as a starting point.

AI tools like A&GEL are not mind-readers; they are pattern-matchers. If you ask a vague question, you will get a generic answer. If you provide rich, specific context, you are much more likely to get a precise, actionable result.

Let’s call this starting point “context.”

Think of context as the raw material A&GEL works with. When drafting a legal memo, don’t just say, “Summarize this case.” Instead, upload the judgment, identify the key factual or legal issues you are concerned with, and tell the AI who the audience is (or you could use pre-made prompts which we have developed for summarising which can be found in the Prompt Library). As a general rule, the more context you provide, the better A&GEL performs.

AI tools like A&GEL empower you directly—they amplify your capabilities, but only if you provide the direction. Without context, the AI is like a brilliant lawyer who hasn’t been briefed on the facts, or a capable colleague who lacks the background information needed to do the job.

Here are 3 takeaways from this habit
  1. Giving A&GEL specific documents before you prompt – If you drag and drop a file onto chat or select a file and chat with it, this is like loading A&GEL’s short-term memory only with the contents of that that document. Many of you who attended our workshops would have heard us describing the file as the ‘K-drama episode you just watched last night’ (it is all you want to talk about today). This makes A&GEL have tunnel vision and can be channeled for accurate outputs.
  2. Working with A&GEL is not unlike working with a colleague – just as you would give a team member some information to help them along the way, it is the same with A&GEL.
  3. What about the query or instruction in my prompt itself? Does that count as ‘giving something to A&GEL?’ Yes. To AI tools like A&GEL, the prompt is ingested/processed just as a series of words, so this means the entire prompt is just ‘context’ for the AI tool or A&GEL. If you are asking a question in your prompt, ask better questions and you will get better answers.

Habit 02

Use LLMs as “Text Processors”, Not Knowledge Engines

It is crucial to understand what Large Language Models (LLMs) actually are. They are not databases of truth, nor are they substitutes for human judgment. Instead, think of an LLM as a high-speed CPU or processor for language and basic cognitive tasks.

An LLM excels at structuring information, identifying patterns, drafting text, and organising complex data. It is a tool for processing, not for deciding. You remain the judge, the strategist, and the final authority. The AI handles the heavy lifting of language manipulation—rephrasing, summarizing, extracting clauses—while you apply your professional expertise to validate the output. This distinction is vital: trust the AI to process, but never outsource your judgment. As the guide reminds us, AI tools “may not replace human judgment, but in many ways, they amplify your capabilities.”

Some Takeaways
Break it down (especially long prompts)

Many users treat A&GEL like a simple chatbot, typing one long prompt and hoping for the best. This is not the best way to use AI tools like A&GEL. Advanced users break down complex tasks into smaller, manageable ones – so instead of one large prompt, have several sub-prompts. This technique, known as decomposition, often leads to higher quality results.

Habit 03

Don’t Just Chat—Explore other features

Intro

By exploring features beyond simple chat—such as uploading documents, using specific templates, or iterating through steps—you gain granular control over the output.

Experimentation is key. Please experiment and explore the other features freely and try things out. Many of the features under the Specialised Modes are tuned for more accuracy and thoroughness. Discover which features work best for your workflow, and don’t be afraid to make mistakes. Skill-building is like climbing a staircase; start with the basics, and as you grow more confident, you will naturally move to more advanced techniques.

By mastering context, respecting the AI’s role as a processor, and breaking down complex tasks, you will transform A&GEL from a novelty into an indispensable part of your daily practice.

Now put it into practice. Here's what you're working with.

Chat with context

Ask, draft, and summarise — all grounded in your own files. Zero context means fast public-knowledge answers; a higher preset draws on your team’s actual precedents.

E.G. 0% context for proofreading; a tailored preset for matter-specific work.

Your Vault

Four tiers: Personal, Group, Firmwide, and Public. Good folder structure is metadata — a well-organised Vault tells you at a glance whether a document is the right one for your matter.

E.G. Press the Chat icon on any file or folder to work in exactly that context.

Prompting

Every effective prompt has four parts: Persona, Task, Context, and Format. Context is where your expertise comes in — a prompt without it is a guess.

E.G. Specify the legal framework, the commercial facts, and what “good” looks like. The more specific, the more useful the output.
